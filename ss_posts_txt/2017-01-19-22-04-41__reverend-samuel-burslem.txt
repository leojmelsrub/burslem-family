Matriculation: He matriculated on 17 Apr 1719. (1)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 2. He received a degree of B.A. on 21 Feb 1723 from the University of Oxford, Brasenose College. (2)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 3. He was ordained a Priest on 4 Jul 1725 in Eccleshall, Staffordshire.&nbsp;The Ordination was by Edward Charles, Bishop of Coventry and Lichfield. (3</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 4. He was Curate of the Fradswell Chapel on 5 Jul 1725 in Colwich, Staffs.Concurrently he was also Curate of Milwich, Staffs. &nbsp; (3)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 5. He received a degree of M.A. on 5 Jul 1726 from the University of Oxford,&nbsp;&nbsp;Brasenose College. (4)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 6. He was a schoolmaster in 1729 in Drayton in Hales, Shropshire. (5) He was appointed on the resignation of the Chief Master, John Addenbroke.</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; His most famous pupil was Robert Clive, afterwards Lord Clive of India.</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; The following extract was found in the book <em>"Lord Clive". " .......... His (Robert Clive's) school life commenced when he was very young at Dr. Eaton's &nbsp;private school at Lostocke in Cheshire. At the age of eleven he was removed &nbsp;to the care of the Rev. Mr. Burslem at Market Drayton with whom he remained &nbsp;for few years.................." . In a book by George Forrest( The life </em><em>&nbsp;of Lord Clive, published by Cassell in 1918) there was the following </em><em>reference " From Lostock, clive returned to Drayton, where he was placed </em><em>&nbsp;under the control of Mr. Burslem, the master of the grammar school, who was </em><em>eminent in the Latin tongue" .</em> Clive was ultimately expelled.</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 7. In Sep 1730 he was Vicar in Drayton in Hales, Shropshire. His patron was Robert Corbett, Bart. (3)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 8. He was Rector 12 Jun 1735 to 8 Mar 1747 in Norton in Hales, Shropshire.&nbsp; His patron was Rowland Cotton. (6)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; An article in the Antiquary Magazine describing the parish register of Norton - in - Hales has the following information:-</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; &nbsp;&nbsp; "An inset, about post octavo in size, gives a list of the rectors. It was drawn up by the Rev. Lawrence Dundas Henry Cokbume, and the succeeding names were added by the &nbsp;present rector. It will be observed that Joshua Bennion, 1650, does not &nbsp;appear on the record :</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; Peter Stringer . . . Buried 30th Oct. 1577</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp; Alan Downes 1594 -1603</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; William Prymrose, Instituted and Inducted, 19 Oct. 1611</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; William Homersley 1635-8</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; Moses Leigh 1650</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; John Bradley 1679</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; William Sorton 1679 ,Buried December 31, 1734.</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; Samuel Burslem I735, Vacated by promotion, 1747-</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; ........"</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; and</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; "the last official entry is as follows :</p><p class="text-align-center">&nbsp;&nbsp; June 25, 1736.</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; Thes was the Register exhibited in to this place at the Visitation of ye right rev. Father in God, Richard, Ld. Bishop of Lichfield &amp; Coventry.</p><p>&nbsp;Sam. Burslem, Rector.&nbsp;</p><p>Robert Parkinson, Churchwarden. "</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 9. He was appointed Vicar on 18 Jun 1746 in Drayton in Hales, Shropshire.&nbsp; (3)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 10. He was Vicar on 21 Nov 1747 in Etwall, Derbyshire. (3) His Patron was Rowland Cotton. (3)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 11. In 1758 he subscribed to Thoughts Moral and Divine (Second edn.), 1758,&nbsp; CALCOTT, Wellins. Birmingham (7)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 12. In 1767 he Subscribed to The Thebaid of Statius, Translated Into English Verse, With Notes And Observations; And A Dissertation upon the whole by Way of Preface., 1767, LEWIS, William Lillington. Oxford. Printed At The Clarendon-Press. M DCC LXVII. (8)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp; 13. He was Master of Etwall Hospital in 1767 in Etwall, Derbyshire.</p><p>--------------------------------------------------------------------------------</p><p><strong>Parents</strong></p><p>--------------------------------------------------------------------------------</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Father: Thomas Burslem (Abt 1694-Abt 1742)</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Mother:</p><p>--------------------------------------------------------------------------------</p><p><strong>Spouses and Children</strong></p><p>--------------------------------------------------------------------------------</p><p>1.</p><p>*Sarah (&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; - 27 Jul 1760)</p><p>Â </p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Marriage:</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Children:</p><p>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; 1.
